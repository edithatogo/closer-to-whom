# Publication strategy

## Paper 1 — clinically focused NZMJ article

The first paper should present a frozen national analysis while retaining a concise Northern historical motivation. The clinical question is whether alternative anti-HER2 delivery configurations change cumulative travel and cost, who is expected to benefit or lose, and what capacity they imply.

Core content: national service census, aggregate demand, central/district/nearest IV comparisons, hospital/community/home SC and hybrid scenarios, distributional and equity outcomes, implied capacity, principal DSA/PSA, and a concise VOI result.

## Paper 2 — decision science and methods

National robust optimisation, multiobjective frontiers, stochastic MCDA, detailed structural uncertainty, EVPPI/EVSI/ENBS, and the value of microdata-level research belong in a separate health-services, operations-research, or health-economics paper.

## Optional paper 3 — data/software

The living service registry, public data contracts, reproducibility system, and model laboratory may support a software or data paper after the registry reaches a stable release.

The open dashboard and technical report are supplements, not substitutes for clinically interpretable primary results.
