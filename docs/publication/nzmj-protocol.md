# NZMJ-oriented protocol

## Working title

**Closer to whom? Modelled access, equity, travel-cost, and infrastructure effects of alternative anti-HER2 treatment delivery configurations in Aotearoa New Zealand**

## Design

Nationwide public-data, aggregate geospatial counterfactual policy simulation with a systematic public-source service census, distributional cost-consequence analysis, uncertainty analysis, and value-of-information component.

## Population

Expected annual treatment courses for clinically distinct early and metastatic HER2-positive pathways, estimated from public aggregate population and incidence inputs and calibrated to public totals.

## Comparators

Central IV, domicile-district IV, nearest eligible IV, optimised satellite IV, travel support, hospital SC trastuzumab, hospital/satellite PHESGO, community SC, healthcare-professional home SC, hybrid pathways, capacity envelopes, and site-outage scenarios.

## Co-primary outcomes

1. expected cumulative door-to-door travel minutes per course;
2. expected direct patient and whānau travel cost per course.

## Principal distributional outcome

Expected proportion and magnitude of demand made better, similar, and worse off under each scenario.

## Equity

Māori, Pacific peoples, deprivation, rurality, age, vehicle access, and disability/mobility proxies where public aggregate data permit. Absolute and relative gaps, tails, and worst-served demand are reported with ecological limitations.

## Operational outputs

Expected administrations, chair/space hours, active nursing hours, provider travel, peak-equivalent workload, resilience, and capacity envelopes. These are implied requirements, not observed capacity.

## Analysis

Network routing, assignment rules, robust multiobjective location-allocation, DSA, PSA, structural scenarios, stochastic MCDA, and VOI. Primary results remain disaggregated; scalar value functions are confined to declared decision analyses.

## Ethics and data

No participants, contact, intervention, linkage, confidential records, or individual data. Applicable ethics-scope and institutional determinations will be obtained and reported before submission.
