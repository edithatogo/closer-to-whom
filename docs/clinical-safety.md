# Clinical safety and pathway governance

This is a service-configuration model, not a medicine-administration protocol. Clinical feasibility is represented through explicit pathway constraints reviewed before publication.

## Hard constraints

- medicine, indication, formulation, and funding status are pathway specific;
- IV trastuzumab, standalone SC trastuzumab, and fixed-dose pertuzumab–trastuzumab are not interchangeable labels;
- initial or higher-risk administrations remain in an appropriately capable setting where required;
- SC home scenarios are healthcare-professional administered, not self-administration;
- resuscitation, observation, cold-chain, prescribing, pharmacy, documentation, and escalation requirements are retained;
- concurrent chemotherapy, cardiac monitoring, oncology review, investigations, and other non-localisable visits remain in the course burden;
- safety and regulatory feasibility are never weighted away through MCDA.

## Clinical review gate

Before publication, every pathway record requires:

1. source freeze date;
2. indication and funding status;
3. loading and maintenance schedule;
4. administration and observation resources;
5. initial and subsequent setting rules;
6. concomitant treatment and monitoring visits;
7. explicit community/home feasibility state;
8. clinical reviewer and date;
9. unresolved ambiguity log.
