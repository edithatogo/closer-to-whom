# Closer to whom?

This project asks whether administratively “local” cancer treatment is actually closer, cheaper, and more equitable for the populations expected to use it.

The open phase is an **ex-ante, aggregate, public-data policy evaluation**. It compares centralised IV treatment, district assignment, nearest eligible services, additional satellites, hospital subcutaneous treatment, community administration, healthcare-professional home administration, hybrid pathways, travel support, and resilience scenarios.

!!! warning "Interpretation"
    The model estimates potential access under assumptions. It does not contain individual records or estimate observed journeys, preferences, queues, capacity, treatment completion, or outcomes.

The primary reporting discipline is distributional: who is expected to be better off, who is worse off, by how much, under which assumptions, and with what implied provider resources. Mean distance alone is insufficient.
