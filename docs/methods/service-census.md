# Systematic public-source census of cancer services

## Review question

Which New Zealand facilities are publicly documented, as of the freeze date, to provide systemic anti-cancer therapy relevant to the model, with what drug/formulation specificity, delivery setting, opening pattern, and evidence strength?

## Sources

Search official Health New Zealand national, regional, district, hospital, service, and archived pages; Te Aho o Te Kahu; Pharmac; Medsafe; facility-code/HPI tables; official service plans and reports; published OIA responses; peer-reviewed service descriptions; and Healthpoint only under its applicable access and redistribution terms.

## Search procedure

Search each region, district, hospital, and locality with oncology, medical oncology, chemotherapy, SACT, targeted therapy, biological therapy, infusion, day unit, trastuzumab, Herceptin, pertuzumab, PHESGO, outreach, satellite, home, and community terms. Record the query, engine, date, result set, reviewer, and adjudication.

## Evidence grades

1. Current official source explicitly confirms the named treatment/formulation at the named site.
2. Current official source confirms solid-tumour SACT, targeted therapy, or chemotherapy but not the named medicine.
3. Oncology clinic, outreach, day unit, or ambiguous capability is documented.
4. Historical, indirect, third-party, or weak evidence.

The primary network uses a frozen conservative threshold. Structural analyses progressively include weaker evidence. No-public-evidence remains unknown.

## Dual review and disagreement

Clinically material Grade 1/2 records receive independent review. Disagreements are resolved by a third adjudication and retained in the audit trail. Inter-reviewer agreement is reported, but agreement never substitutes for evidence quality.

## Living update

The census is versioned by freeze date. Automated monitors may identify changed pages, but every change is re-adjudicated before entering a model release.
