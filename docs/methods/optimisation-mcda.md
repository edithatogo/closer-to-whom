# Optimisation and MCDA

## Optimisation

The model supports:

- p-median: minimise expected total burden;
- p-centre/minimax: minimise worst or near-worst burden;
- maximal coverage: maximise expected demand within a travel threshold;
- robust optimisation: perform well across demand, facility, route, adoption, and outage uncertainty;
- multiobjective optimisation: expose the Pareto frontier for access, equity, provider resources, resilience, and implementation burden.

Without observed capacity, the primary question is where potential access would be improved and what capacity that configuration would imply. Artificial low, medium, and high envelopes are sensitivity analyses, not descriptions of current operations.

## MCDA

Optimisation creates an efficient shortlist. MCDA then considers criteria that should not be collapsed casually into money. Safety and regulatory feasibility remain hard constraints outside the score.

Without a formal stakeholder elicitation, no single weight set is called societal. The model reports declared viewpoint profiles and stochastic multicriteria acceptability over broad weight distributions: first-rank probabilities, expected rank, and the weight regions that reverse a conclusion.
